How did we build the yougig training web and mobile app?

Understanding our client’s vision was the key to the whole process, as we had a chance to create an application that was meant to revolutionize how professional yougig app & web organize the training of young adepts. Therefore, the application delivery was divided into a few phases, described below.

Product Design Workshops

The client had an excellent idea for the product but required complete technical advisory from our side. During the sales process, we recommended a solution for the application. We prepared a preliminary Product Architecture described in detail during our Product Design Workshop sessions with the client.

Based on the conclusions from the workshop, we defined the core scope of the project and prioritized milestones and features. Finally, we have created a complete product roadmap with a detailed estimation of each milestone.

UX/UI Design

Designing a user-centric UX/UI involves a blend of creativity, empathy, and technical expertise to create a visually appealing, functional, and delightful experience for users interacting with websites or applications.

We started the UX design of the mobile application during the Product Design Workshops phase. We decided to start the UX/UI Design process from the Flutter mobile application User Interface. We worked in Design Sprints as the final idea was evolving during the process. As usual, we started with wireframes. Then, with the client’s feedback, they transformed them into low-fi and hi-fi mockups with micro animations and motion design.

Agile Development

We implemented and delivered the system in six months, while the expectations were to create the whole system in ten months.

During the Agile Development, we have followed standard Agile methodology.

As a result, we have created the following deliverables

  • User interface and experience designs, including wireframes, mockups, and style guides.
  • Graphics, icons, and visual elements are designed for use within the software.
  • Repositories housing the source code, allowing for version control and collaborative development.
  • Documentation of identified bugs, issues, and their resolutions
  • Functional models showcasing the software's core features and user interactions.

Maintenance

We are responsible for the maintenance of the system. The maintenance phase’s goal is to ensure that the application is up-to-date and attractive.

During the maintenance, we are responsible for:

  • Implementing patches or updates to fix bugs, improve performance, or enhance security.
  • Regular inspections to identify and address potential issues or system inefficiencies.
  • informing users about maintenance schedules, updates, and potential disruptions.
  • Keeping records of maintenance activities, changes made, and issues resolved.
  • Identifying and patching security vulnerabilities to safeguard against potential threats.
